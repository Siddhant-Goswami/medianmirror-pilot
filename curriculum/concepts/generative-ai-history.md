## Definition
The 80-year arc from mathematical neuron models (1940s) to modern generative AI systems (2022+). Understanding this history reveals why current AI works the way it does and how quickly the capability-adoption gap can close.

## Why It Matters
Most people assume AI is 30-40 years old. It is 80+ years old. The field has gone through multiple hype cycles and winters. Understanding the arc builds intuition for where we actually are now — and why the current moment is genuinely different from prior hype cycles.

## How It Works (The Arc)

**1940s — The biological origin:**
- McCulloch-Pitts Neuron Model: single biological neuron (dendrites → axon → synapse) modeled with transistors
- Chained together = first artificial neural network
- Original goal: simulate human cognition, not build chatbots

**1950s — Turing Test:**
- Alan Turing (father of CS and AI): "machines can think and act faster than humans"
- WWII contribution: broke the Enigma cipher, arguably deciding the war
- Turing Test: can you distinguish machine from human in conversation?
- Current status: ~70% of the time, humans cannot distinguish AI from human

**1950s-1990s — Cycles and winters:**
- Repeated pattern: breakthrough → hype → funding → diminishing returns → winter → rebuild
- Each winter was caused by hitting the ceiling of the current architectural paradigm

**2010s — Deep learning resurgence:**
- GPU computing makes large neural networks tractable
- ImageNet competition (2012): AlexNet deep neural net massively outperforms all prior approaches
- Foundation for modern computer vision and eventually LLMs

**2017 — Transformer architecture:**
- "Attention Is All You Need" (Google) — attention mechanism enables parallelization and long-range context
- Foundation for all modern LLMs

**2022 — ChatGPT and the current moment:**
- Public inflection point. LLMs become consumer-accessible.
- The gap between capability and human adoption opened wide
- ~70% of time: humans can't distinguish AI from human in conversation

**Why now is different (Sridev's argument):**
- Even if AI development froze today, 4-5 years needed to extract value from current toolset
- Early adopter window: still open for 1-1.5 years
- AI is domain-agnostic — the question is "what does AI do to MY domain?"

## Key Variants / Extensions
- **Discriminative AI**: classifies or predicts (old paradigm)
- **Generative AI**: creates new content — text, images, video, code
- **Foundation models**: large pre-trained models fine-tuned for specific tasks (GPT, Claude, FLUX, etc.)
